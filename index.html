What is the operating cash flow?
the operating cash flow (OCF) is a measurement of the net cash generated from the core functions of a company within a specified time period.
How to calculate operating cash flow?
To calculate operating cash flow you should use the following formula:
operating cash flow (OCF) = net income + depreciation and a mortization - increase in net working capital
example: company x has $3,800,000 in net income and the depreciation and a mortization is $970,000 and a $500,000 increase in net working capital, calculate the operating cash flow of the company?
using the operating cash flow formula the answer will be:
operating cash flow = net income + depreciation and a mortization - increase in net working capital
operating cash flow = $3,800,000 + $970,000 - $500,000
operating cash flow = $4,270,000
Why use operating cash flow?
the operating cash flow is used to determine the financial success of a firm's core business activities because it measures the amount of cash generated by a company's normal business operations.
